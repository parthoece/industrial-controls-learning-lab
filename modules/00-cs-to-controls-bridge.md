# Module 0 — CS-to-Controls Bridge

## Outcomes

- map familiar CS concepts to industrial-control responsibilities
- explain why timing, units, and physical energy change software design
- distinguish command acceptance from physical result
- trace a production instruction to motion and telemetry

## Reuse from CS

- state machines and finite-state modeling
- interfaces, dependency inversion, and test doubles
- producer/consumer queues and concurrency boundaries
- databases, events, APIs, schemas, and migrations
- tests, CI, logging, metrics, and version control

## New mental models

- timing is part of correctness
- units and coordinate systems are part of the API contract
- faults are expected operating states
- physical outputs may carry hazardous energy
- feedback can be stale, noisy, wrong, or mechanically inconsistent
