# Module 3 review — Motion and networks

Answer without opening the weekly lessons.

1. List the diagnostic layers from permission to mechanics.

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Permission, network, drive state, axis state, command lifecycle, mechanics/model.

</details>

2. What does an unexpected EtherCAT working counter mean?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Expected datagram processing did not occur; investigate device presence, state, mapping, and access.

</details>

3. Why is drive operation enabled not enough to move?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Machine permission, coordinate validity, target validity, command state, and mechanics are separate.

</details>

4. Calculate stopping distance at 2 m/s with 4 m/s² deceleration.

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

0.5 m.

</details>

5. What makes a position valid?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Established coordinate reference, valid feedback, known frame, and acceptable quality/freshness.

</details>
