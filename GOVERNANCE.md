# Governance

## Model

The project begins maintainer-led and may grow into a small reviewer group.

## Roles

- **Contributor:** submits issues, reviews, documentation, code, tests, or translations.
- **Reviewer:** has demonstrated reliable judgment in one or more project areas.
- **Maintainer:** can merge changes, manage releases, moderate spaces, and set priorities.

## Decisions

Routine changes are decided through pull-request review. Significant changes—license changes, curriculum restructuring, public API changes, new governance, or removal of a supported learning path—start with a proposal issue.

Maintainers seek consensus. When consensus is not possible, active maintainers decide and record the rationale in the issue or an architecture decision record.

## Role changes

Reviewer and maintainer roles require sustained constructive participation, technical accuracy, safety awareness, respectful review, and reliable follow-through. Existing maintainers approve changes publicly.

## Conflicts of interest

Participants disclose vendor, employment, or financial interests that could materially affect a decision and step back when appropriate.

## Inactivity

A maintainer inactive for six months may be moved to emeritus status after reasonable contact attempts. Returning maintainers follow the normal role process.
